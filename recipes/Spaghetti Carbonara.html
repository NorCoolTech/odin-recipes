Spaghetti Carbonara

Home

Description

Spaghetti Carbonara is a traditional Italian pasta dish made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper. The creamy sauce comes from the eggs and cheese — no cream needed! It’s quick, rich, and satisfying, perfect for a comforting dinner.

Originating from Rome, this dish is loved worldwide for its simplicity and incredible flavor.

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 150g pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 3 large eggs
  • 75g grated Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 tbsp olive oil

Steps

  1. Cook the spaghetti in salted boiling water according to the package instructions.
  2. In a pan, heat olive oil and cook the pancetta until crispy.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, cheese, and black pepper.
  4. Drain the pasta, reserving a bit of the cooking water.
  5. Add the pasta to the pancetta, remove from heat, then quickly mix in the egg mixture.
  6. Toss well, adding a splash of pasta water if needed for creaminess.
  7. Serve immediately with extra cheese and black pepper on top.
